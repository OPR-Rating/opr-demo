Levels Explained

OPR & Playtomic — how they compare

A quick, honest guide for players who know their Playtomic level and want to understand where they sit on OPR.

The quick answer

The rough conversion

OPR runs on its own scale — built for doubles, and finer-grained than Playtomic's 0–7.

As a rough guide, an OPR level sits about 5.5 to 6 points above the equivalent Playtomic level:

OPR level ≈ Playtomic + 5.5
So an OPR 9.6 is around a Playtomic 4.

It's a guide, not a strict formula — and that's deliberate. No two rating systems convert exactly (even Playtomic says its level doesn't convert cleanly to other systems). Giving a range is the honest, accurate way to do it.

Why it's a guide, not a formula

What the numbers show

Here are three real players across both systems:

PlayerOPR levelPlaytomic
Player A9.64.0
Player B9.73.45
Player C7.92.0

Look at A and B. OPR has them almost level — 9.6 and 9.7. Playtomic has them half a point apart, in the opposite order. They can't both be right — and the reason matters.

OPR has watched A and B play a full body of matches at the club, so it knows they're the same standard. The Playtomic figures come from only a handful of logged rounds — and Playtomic itself says a level isn't reliable until a player has played ~15–20 matches. So the gap wanders not because OPR is uncertain, but because the Playtomic numbers are still settling.

Why OPR is the truer read at your club

Built on your real history

  • Full match history. OPR is built on every match a player has actually played at the club — not a handful of app-logged games.
  • Finer resolution. Where a broad 0–7 scale lumps players into half-point bands, OPR can separate two players who'd both just show as "a 4" — exactly what you need inside a club where everyone's a similar standard.
  • Built for doubles. OPR rates how pairs actually perform together, which is how club padel is really played.
  • It's your club's number. The OPR level is the real standing where a player actually plays — not a global average from elsewhere.
How to put it

The friendly one-liner

When someone says "but we use Playtomic"

"Great — roughly, take about 5.5 off an OPR level and you're in Playtomic territory, so a 9.6 is around a Playtomic 4. But OPR's a sharper instrument: it can tell two similar players apart that a broad global scale, on limited rounds, gets in the wrong order — because OPR is reading your actual club history."
