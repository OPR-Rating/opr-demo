Form
What is Form? Your overall performance — not just how often you win, but who you played, how convincingly, and how by much. Requires a minimum of 12 sets to appear.

A quick guide to what each column means.

The columns explained

  • Player / Pair — the player's name, or the pairing.
  • Sets — total sets played (must reach 12 to appear).
  • Sets Won — how many of those sets were won.
  • Win % — share of sets won.
  • Game Diff / Set — average game margin per set. 3.57 means winning sets by about 3–4 games on average. Positive = usually wins convincingly; negative = usually loses.
  • Form — the overall performance score, 0.0–1.0 (read as 0–100%). The headline number the wall is sorted by.

Reading Form (0.0 – 1.0)

It blends how often you win sets, your game margin per set, and your games won per set — then adjusts for how many sets you've played (more = more reliable). Rough guide:

  • 0.70+ — Strong, performing very well.
  • 0.55–0.70 — Good, winning more than losing.
  • 0.40–0.55 — Middling, competitive but mixed.
  • Below 0.40 — Struggling.

Why Form, not just Win %?

Win % treats a narrow 7–6 win the same as a crushing 6–0, and ignores who you played. Form rewards convincing performances and accounts for opponent strength — a fairer picture of how someone is actually playing. For doubles, Form shows how a partnership performs as a team.

    About OPR

    Open Padel Rating

    Designed for All Sorts of Combined pairings

    What Is OPR?

    Open Padel Rating (OPR) is an independent, transparent rating system designed specifically for doubles and mixed pairs padel. Unlike existing systems that adapt individual tennis or squash models, OPR was built from the ground up for the realities of club padel — where partners change, gender combinations vary, and match volume is limited.

    OPR is open by design. Every formula, every weighting, and every adjustment threshold is explainable to any player, administrator, or partner. There are no black boxes.

    How Ratings Work

    Every match updates your rating automatically. The system considers not just whether you won, but how convincingly you won — set margins, game differentials, and how surprising the result was given the teams' ratings.

    Bigger upsets earn more points. Dominant victories earn more than narrow wins. Playing more matches builds confidence in your rating over time.

    Match Types

    Men's Ladies Mixed All Sorts

    OPR handles any combination — two men, two women, mixed gender, or any All Sorts configuration. Mixed and All Sorts pairs use a weighted rating that gives more weight to the stronger partner, reflecting their greater influence on the match.

    Your Level

    Your Level is simply your rating divided by 200. It gives you a simple number to communicate your standard without needing to explain raw rating points. Level 5 is entry, Level 9 is elite.
